Research Experience

2018- 2019

IISER Tirupati.

Theoretical Investigation of Magnetism at Different Length Scales.

Project Assistant.

Understanding the static and dynamic properties of a magnet at mesoscopic scale is necessary for the applications of magnets in various magneto-electronics devices, non-volatile memory devices and magnetic sensors. The magnetisation reversal and coercive mechanism for such magnets are studied statistically using Landau-Liftshitz-Gilbert equation in presence of exchange, anisotropy, zeeman & dipole-dipole interaction. Threshold fields for nucleation and domain wall propagation are estimated. The effect of pinning and nucleation mechanism are being observed in presence of defects. The effect of temperature in such processes are also explored theoretically.

2017- 2018

IISER Tirupati.

Soliton Propagation Through Asymmetric Dual-Core Waveguide.

Project Assistant.

The existence of exact solitons in a non-linear medium with refractive index, gain-loss profile and dispersion is explored analytically and numerically. The key role of third order dispersion in solitons is observed. Also linear stability of these solitons have been investigated.

2014- 2015

IIT Kharagpur.

X-Ray Reflectivity to Probe Material Surfaces and Interfaces.

M. Sc. dissertation project.

X-ray reflectivity (XRR) is a well-established, powerful tool for measuring properties of thin films. This project outlines XRR and its properties in thin films using mathematical tools to simulate an outcome with given conditions. We use the condition variables such as roughness (σ) , thickness (d), density(ρ) and also number of layers to simulate the XRR of organic semi- conducting multiyear thin films . Also a Non-linear Least-squares fitting programme has been created to extract the condition variables from an experimental XRR data of such thin films.
