statement of research

The activities of each organism on earth have a critical impact on the delicate balance of the ecosystem.The interactions among the living entities on our planet have always fascinated me since I my education in Botany for Bachelor’s degree. During my post-graduation course in Plant Biology and Biotechnology, I aimed for my research to be a comprehensive integration of both fundamental knowledge and its applications. My interest in microbial diversity was first kindled during the dissertation project for the Master’s program, when I investigated the diversity of anammox Planctomycetes across the eastern regions of India. Planctomycetes are an enigmatic group of bacteria with high potential in biotechnological applications as their activities are critical in the global carbon and nitrogen cycle. The anammox Planctomycetes have the capability to convert ammonia and nitrite to dinitrogen.During my first year of PhD, I got the opportunity to deal with the “difficult to cultivate” members of Planctomycetes including anammox bacteria. Prior academic work had helped me develop various technical skills in microbial culturing and characterization, imaging, and computational skills. I also nurtured soft skills like scientific thinking and communication. Previous research experiences had given me excellent leads in the topic of association of algae and sponge with planctomycetes and anammox bacteria . This resulted in my contribution as a co-author in two peer -reviewed publications and a few more which are in the pipeline. The exploration of anammox diversity is very important especially considering increasing concerns for the development of energy-efficient and carbon-dependent nitrogen removal technology for mainstream wastewater treatment. Due to these reasons, I find myself motivated to work in this area using both culture- dependent and independent strategies in order to study these bacteria more intimately. Algae being known as one of the best habitats for Planctomycetes, I would like to focus on algal associated anammox diversity. The importance of this work can be explained from the very fact that not even a single anammox bacterium has been isolated in pure culture anywhere in the world. Also, not many labs are working on Planctomycetes and not many anammox bioreactors are operating in India to treat wastewater.This is a highly underexplored research area in India from a biogeographic and biotechnological point of view. When given the chance I hope this work can represent India at the frontiers of anammox research globally. Recognition through the prestigious PMRF scheme will greatly benefit in bringing this field out of its infancy as well as in encouraging others to bring more research focus to it. In summary, I truly believe that, this fellowship can boost my confidence to venture new challenges in anammox research which would in the long term, result in high impact factor publications, and subsequently formulating effective microbial consortia for sustainable and energy-efficient wastewater treatment.
